A design preview for Pam

A quieter, warmer public website for The Mahjong Studio.

Six redesigned pages, built around your feedback and Regina's notes — fewer words, plainer language, every "contact the studio" really a link, and the studio's own photos given proper room. Open each page below to review. Tell me what to keep, change, or scrap and I'll iterate.

01 / HOME

The front door

A calm welcome, three honest ways to start, and one small "ask the studio" link for visitors who aren't sure.

 02 / LEARN

Classes

Plain-language pathway for beginners and returning players, no "open for enrollment" or other system phrases.

 03 / PLAY

Open play & sessions

The sessions themselves are the answer to "how do I choose" — no separate decision grid above them.

 04 / MEMBERSHIP

Membership

Two plain tiers, what's included, who each is for, one clear way to join. No status-machine copy.

 05 / HELP

Help

Organized by the questions visitors actually ask, not by internal audience categories.

 06 / CONTACT

Contact

Email, phone, address, and a short form — front and center. No "look somewhere else instead" deflection.

 07 / ABOUT

About + first visit

Studio backstory, a four-step "what your first visit is like" walkthrough, and a plain facts panel for hours, parking, and accessibility.

How to review: open each page, read it as a visitor would, and tell me what feels right or wrong — copy, layout, photos, what's missing. These are static mockups, so links inside the pages mostly don't lead anywhere yet. Nothing here is wired up to the real site.
